Essential Travel Tips

Transportation

Consider getting a Japan Rail Pass before your trip if you plan to travel between cities. Inside cities, use IC cards like Suica or PASMO for convenient travel on trains and buses. Tokyo's subway system may seem complex but is highly efficient and clean.

Money Matters

Japan is still largely a cash-based society. Always carry cash with you. ATMs at 7-Eleven stores and post offices accept international cards. Credit cards are increasingly accepted at hotels and larger establishments.

Internet Access

Consider renting a pocket WiFi or getting a temporary SIM card upon arrival. Free WiFi is available in many public places but having your own connection is more reliable.

Language Barrier

English is not widely spoken outside major tourist areas. Download a translation app, learn basic Japanese phrases, and carry a small phrasebook. Most signs in major cities have English translations.

Cultural Etiquette

Removing Shoes

Always remove your shoes when entering Japanese homes, traditional ryokan inns, and some restaurants and historical sites. Look for shoe racks or slippers at the entrance.

Tipping

Tipping is not customary in Japan and can even be considered rude or confusing. Service charges are typically included in bills at restaurants and hotels.

Bowing

Bowing is the traditional greeting in Japan. As a tourist, a slight bow or nod of the head is appropriate. Handshakes are becoming more common in business settings.

Public Behavior

Maintain a quiet demeanor in public spaces. Avoid eating while walking, speaking loudly on public transportation, or making phone calls on trains.
